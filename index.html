Violent Crime is Decreasing in Unincorporated Sacramento County


Link to high resolution map showing all streets and major road names

Authored by Tim Kohaya for University of Kentucky New Maps Plus Class 671

Like other urban areas in the U.S., the urbanized portion of unincorporated Sacramento County (population â€“ 500,000) has its share of violent crime incidents. However, the number of violent crimes have gone down in Sacramento County during the last couple of decades and these maps show that this downward trend has continued during the last decade.

The top map shows the number of violent crime incidents per one-half mile hexagon during the 2008-2009 period. Violent crimes include murder, rape, sexual assaults, assaults and robberies. The middle map shows the density of violent crime incidents during the 2016-2017 period. Finally, the bottom map shows the difference between the two time periods. Green-tinted hexagons show areas that have a reduction in violent crimes while red-tinted hexagons shows areas that have an increase. As can be seen, most areas have a reduction of violent crimes.

The maps were created using QGIS. A hexagon grid layer was created using the MMQGIS plugin and then spatially joined with a layer that has data points (violent crime incidents). The resulting new layer contained counts of data points for each hexagon.

Data Source: Sacramento County Open Data Hub https://data-sacramentocounty.opendata.arcgis.com/

Coordinate Reference System (CRS): NAD 83/California Zone 3 (ft.U.S.) (ESPG: 2227).
