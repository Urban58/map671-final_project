Map for the “Play, Eat, Walk” Program in Davis, CA.

Authored by: Tim Kohaya

The above map shows selected amenities that are within one mile of a community park (larger service area and facilities than neighborhood parks). Selected amenities are eating establishments such as cafes and restaurants, theaters, and cinemas. This analysis was done for the “Play, Eat, Walk” nationwide program sponsored by the Institute of Environmental Recreation. This program encourages people to use public parks more during their daily activities because of the physical and social benefits that public parks provide.

Davis’s local “Play, Eat, Walk” program will establish walking clubs that meet at Davis’s community parks. These clubs will take a walk in the park and surrounding neighborhood and end with a meal and (on weekends) a play or a movie. The “Play, Eat, Walk” program will take advantage of Davis’s extensive bike trail system. Davis has more than 50 miles of bike trails and has more bikes per capital than any other city or town in the U.S.

Map Details

This map was developed by using QGIS's QuickOSM tool to retrieve OpenStreetMap features. Geojson files were saved from the temporary QuickOSM files. The geoprocessing tool Fixed Distance Buffer was used to prepare one mile buffers from community parks.
